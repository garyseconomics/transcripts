# How to review a transcript

Thank you for volunteering! Your job is to check that the transcript matches what Gary (and any guests) actually says in the video. An AI has already done a first pass — your fresh ears are what we need to catch anything it got wrong.

## What you'll need

- A web browser (to watch the video and read the transcript)
- Any text editor (Notepad, TextEdit, Google Docs, Word — anything works)

## Steps

1. **Pick a transcript** from the list of `.md` files in this folder. Files in the `done/` subfolder have already been reviewed — skip those.

2. **Open the file** and click the YouTube link at the top. You'll see the transcript below it — this is what the AI thinks Gary said.

3. **Watch the video while reading along.** The timestamps tell you where each paragraph starts, so you can jump to the right spot if you lose your place. Pay attention to the parts in **bold** — those are words the AI changed. If a word has a ~~strikethrough~~, that's what the original auto-generated subtitle said before the AI corrected it.

4. **If everything looks correct**, just let us know — no need to edit anything. A quick "looks good" message is enough.

5. **If you spot any mistakes**, download the file and edit it with your corrections. You can fix it however feels natural — change a word, add a note next to it, leave a comment — whatever is easiest for you. Don't worry about formatting.

6. **Send it back to us** — either by email or on Discord. If you didn't need to make any changes, just let us know which file you reviewed.

## Tips

- Names of people, places, and organisations are the hardest for the AI to get right — pay extra attention to those.
- If you can't quite make out what was said, just flag it with a note like "[unclear]" — that's still really helpful.
- You don't have to review the whole thing in one go. Do as much as you have time for.
